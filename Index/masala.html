Masala Dosa

Masala dosa is a savory, crispy, and delicious South Indian pancake filled with a spiced potato mixture. It's perfect for breakfast or dinner, often served with coconut chutney and tangy sambar. Learn how to make this iconic dish at home!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice (preferably short-grain or idli rice)
  • 1/4 cup split urad dal (skinned black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional)
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Oil or ghee (for cooking)
  • For Masala Filling:
  • 4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Soak the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds (if using) in water for 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain and blend the ingredients with a little water to form a smooth batter.
  3. Allow the batter to ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight.
  4. Once fermented, add salt to taste and stir gently.
  5. For the masala filling: Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, cumin, asafoetida, and saut√© until fragrant.
  6. Add chopped onions and green chilies, and cook until soft.
  7. Stir in turmeric powder, and add the mashed potatoes. Mix well, cook for a few minutes, and then garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  8. Heat a tawa, grease it with oil or ghee, and pour a ladleful of batter, spreading it in a circular motion.
  9. Cook until golden brown, then place a spoonful of the masala filling in the center. Fold the dosa and serve hot.
